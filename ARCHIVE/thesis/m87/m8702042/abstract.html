具公平性特徵選取之智慧型網頁自動分類器

摘要

由於網路上的資訊量不斷地成長，許多網頁搜尋工具陸續提出，它們利用將網頁分門別類，讓使用者能夠透過瀏覽的方式，瀏覽相關的主題，所以發展一個自動分類器是必要的。然而由於網頁資源龐大，幾乎所有的分類器都面臨了動輒數以百萬計的文件，以及數以萬計的輸入維度，更嚴重地，大部分的分類器都忽略了分類速度的重要性並且缺乏學習能力。 針對這些限制，在降低維度的技術中，我們針對特徵選取方法提出一個公平的特徵選取演算法，它可以公平的看待各類別，並且能夠大量降低輸入維度；另外我們亦提出一個「可調適的模糊學習網路架構」( Adaptive Fuzzy Learning Network ) 來實做文章分類，以達到快速分類及學習專家的分類知識的優點。由中時電子報的資料實驗結果可知，運用我們的技術，當輸入維度由24190降至200時，仍然可使測試語料達到86% 的正確率。

An Intelligent Web-Page Classifier with Fair Feature-Subnet Selection

Abstract

The explosion of on-line information has given rise to many manually constructed topic hierarchies (such as Yahoo!!). But, with the current growth rate in the amount of information, manual classification in topic hierarchies creates an immense information bottleneck. Therefore, developing an automatic classifier is an urgent need. However, the classifiers suffer from the enormous dimensionality since the dimensionality is determined by the number of distinct keywords in a document corpus. More seriously, most classifiers are either working slowly or they are constructed subjectively without learning ability. In this thesis, we address these problems with a fair feature subset selection algorithm and an adaptive fuzzy learning network (AFLN) for classification. First, we have ability to reduce the enormous dimensionality significantly using our fair feature subset selection algorithm. Further, we can employ our AFLN to classify unseen documents into existing manually generated hierarchies. Our feature subset selection algorithm is sound theoretical. It not only gives fair treatment to each category but also has ability to identify useful features, including both positive and negative features. On the other hand, the AFLN provides extremely fast training and testing and, more importantly, it has leaning ability to acquire the human knowledge. In short, our methods will allow large amounts of information to be organized and presented to users in a comprehensible way. By alleviating the information bottleneck, we hope to help users with the problems of information access on the Internet.
